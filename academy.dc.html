Why The Academy

Equipping the saints of God for the works of God

Part of the mission of Citipointe Nashville is to equip the saints of God for the works of God. The Academy exists for anyone in the church who wants to passionately pursue Christ in a community seeking to serve Him without compromise — while deepening their understanding of both theology and ministry.

Practically, that happens three ways: Monday night classes, Serve Saturdays once a month, and a personal growth track — partnering with the church or a ministry beyond it in an area that aligns with the gifts God has given you.

How it works

Three ways you'll grow

01 · In the classroom
Monday night classes

Each night is split in two. The first part — about an hour and fifteen minutes — centers on the theological concepts that shape and define the Christian faith, followed by Q&A in a classroom environment. Doctrines like the Trinity, Revelation, and Justification, among many others, explored together as a class.

The second part brings in guest speakers gifted in the aspects of ministry students are seeking to grow in — prophecy, evangelism, and leadership among many others.

02 · In the city
Serve Saturdays

Once a month, students learn outside the classroom in a team environment. Citipointe partners with ministries across the city — Red Frogs and Freedom Collective offer unique environments to evangelize and serve, and the Academy has been serving the homeless by connecting with shelters to provide food.

These are just a few of the ways the group serves the community — developing a love for the lost, and for Jesus.

03 · In your calling
Your growth track

The Academy partners with each student to find an area or track for growth that fits the gifts and calling on their lives — serving with the church, or with a ministry beyond it.

We are believing for each student to reach their God-given destiny. As a group, we want to call out the best and the future God has placed within each of them.

"If you want to go faster, go alone.
If you want to go further, go together."
