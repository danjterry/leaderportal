The leader's win
A healthy lifegroup produces people who are known, cared for, growing in God's Word, and raising up the next leaders.
The model: dinner parties
Every group is a dinner party. The table is the strategy — a meal people want to invite their friends to, where community happens before the study does.
Every group meets Wednesday night. For the next 12 months, all groups gather on one night. One rhythm for the whole church — then we grow from there.
Communion at every gathering. Every table, every week. Pick up Communion resources on Sundays, or midweek from the church office.
Wednesday night run sheet
A guide, not a script — shift the times to suit your table, but protect dinner, Communion, and finishing on time.

Join requests: the 48-hour rule
When someone asks to join through the website or app, respond within 48 hours — a fast, warm hello communicates that we value people. And we never turn anyone away: if your table is at capacity, talk to your Pastoral Oversight about alternatives before saying no.
Before you cancel
For many, Wednesday night is the anchor of their week. Contact your Pastoral Oversight before cancelling a gathering — there's usually an alternative. Same goes for changes to format or frequency. You're not on your own.
You have an oversight — use them
Every leader has a Pastoral Oversight for care, coaching, and any question about discipling your group — build the relationship, don't wait for a crisis. Discipleship Support keeps a pulse on who's engaged so nobody slips through the cracks. Reply to both promptly. Pastoral Oversight: Dan Terry, 628 259 9460.

Discipleship resources
Discipleship is a life transaction — teaching others everything Jesus has taught you. (Matthew 28:18–20)
How to make a disciple
Listen first, then ask questions. Understand where they're coming from; the right question grows people faster than the right answer.
Teach truth and equip them. Break the Word down where they're at — then show them how you study, pray, and practice the disciplines.
Be consistent in their life. Connect outside the group — groceries, hikes, your kitchen table. Discipleship is relationship.
Dig for gold. Help people see themselves the way God sees them, and teach them the importance of being planted in the local church.
Love unconditionally. Give grace and mercy as you address growth — the Holy Spirit changes hearts, not us.
Put in their hands
New believers: the "Following Jesus" book + app (7 essentials, with video course), and the book of John — one chapter a day on the YouVersion Bible app.
Going deeper: Theos U short courses (my.theosu.ca) and the Enduring Word commentary (enduringword.com).
Topical books: "Keep Your Love On" (Danny Silk), "Expanded Leadership" (Mark Ramsey & Karolina Gunsser), and "Secrets of the Secret Place".
